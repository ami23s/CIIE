About the Event

Women 4 Women Conclave, by the Centre of Innovation, Incubation and Entrepreneurship, is a celebration of successful women for various fields, with an aim to inspire and educate a fixed number of 1000 participants including 50 women from rural regions.

With advanced networking programs and stories a plenty to inspire everyone, the programme is aimed at educating anyone and everyone in the ever-growing importance of women in the field of Innovation and Entrepreneurship.

MEET OUR SPEAKERS

Mugdha Sinha

IAS Officer | Secretary, DST, GoR

The first woman officer to be posted as ADM-1 (Additional District Magistrate) in Jaipur. Ms. Mugdha Sinha is currently Secretary of Government in Science and Technology Department, Jaipur.

Gautami Tadimalla

Cine Actress | Cancer Survivor | Social Entrepreneur

Gautami Tadimalla is an Indian film actress who has worked in many Tamil films, along with some Telugu, Malayalam, Hindi and Kannada films. Being a breast cancer survivor herself, She is the founder of Life Again Foundation that extends moral and financial support to the poor cancer patients.

Jaahnavi Sriperambuduru

Young Woman Mountaineer

Jaahnavi Sriperambuduru is a young Indian mountaineer and a motivational speaker. She is on her mission called the “Mission 7 Summit” and holds the record of being the youngest girl to scale Europe's tallest peak and the youngest Indian to climb the highest peak of Australia.

Jyothi Yerolkar Kheny

Senior Wing Commander | Medical doctor, Indian Air Force

Ms. Jyothi Yerolkar Kheny grew up watching the Indian Air Force (IAF) trainer jets fly over her native town of Bidar, she dreamt of joining the IAF as a pilot, aspiring for a life full of adventure.

Shikha Goel

Additional Commissioner of Police

Shikha Goel joined the Indian Forest Service in 1995 and subsequently moved to the Indian Police Service. She has taken up many initiatives like Creating awareness about child sexual abuse, empowering women with initiatives like ‘Women on Wheels’, ‘Bharosa’, and SHE teams are just a few.

Ananda Shankar Jayant

Classical Dancer | Bureaucrat | Breast Cancer Survivor

She is the first woman officer in the Indian Railway Traffic Service. She is a recipient of Sangeet Natak Akademi Award, Kalaimamani Award of the Government of Tamil Nadu and Kala Ratna Award of the Government of Andhra Pradesh. She was awarded the Padma Shri in 2007 for her contributions to the arts.
